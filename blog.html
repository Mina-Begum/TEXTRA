Digital Marketing

Digital marketing typically refers to online marketing campaigns that appear on a computer, phone, tablet, or other device. It includes online video, display ads, search engine marketing, paid social ads, and social media posts.

Unlike traditional marketing like magazine ads and billboards, digital marketing uses the internet to reach users where they spend most of their time. With nearly 90% of U.S. adults online daily, digital strategies are crucial.

It leverages channels like social media, SEO, PPC, and email to grow your brand and reach potential customers. Tools like automation and AI are key, with 88% of marketers recognizing their importance in staying competitive.

Microsoft Excel

Microsoft Excel is a powerful spreadsheet application that helps users organize, analyze, and visualize data using a grid of rows and columns.

Key Features:
- Formulas & Functions (e.g., SUM, VLOOKUP)
- Charts & Graphs (bar, pie, line)
- PivotTables
- Data filtering & sorting
- Macros & VBA scripting
- Conditional Formatting

Front-End Development

A Front-End Developer designs the part of websites users interact with — the client side.

Technologies Used:
- HTML (Structure)
- CSS (Styling)
- JavaScript (Interactivity)

Front-End is what users see, while Back-End is what makes it all work behind the scenes.

Back-End Development

Back-End Development deals with the server-side of applications — where data is stored and processed.

It handles the database, server, APIs, and logic that allow the Front-End to function smoothly.
